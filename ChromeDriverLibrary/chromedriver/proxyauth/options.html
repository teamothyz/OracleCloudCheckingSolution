To enable the extension, enter your proxy login and password in the form.

Once it's done, you should never see any proxy login popup again.

Bad credentials detection
If you make a mistake when setting your credentials, your proxy server will keep sending the authentication request again and again. This may cause you to be blacklisted depending on your proxy rules. That's why the Unsuccessful attempts limit exists. If the authentication request is sent more times than the value you defined, the extension will temporarly auto-lock itself (you'll see a notification) to avoid any issue.
To unlock it, just reset or save your credentials again.

The auto-lock should almost never happen, but if it's the case, try setting a higher value.

Permissions
As you probably saw, Proxy Auto Auth requires the permission to "view your data" on every sites. This permission is just necessary to achieve what this extension does. But be sure that none of your data is stored or used in any way.
